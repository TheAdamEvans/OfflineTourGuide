## Getting Here and Time Needed
- **MRT Blue Line to Sanam Chai Station**: Exit 1, walk past Ministry of Defense building, 7 minutes.
- **Chao Phraya Express Boat**: Tha Chang or Tha Maharaj pier, 5 minute walk.
- **Buses 1, 25, 44** pass Sanam Luang, but avoid 7-9 AM rush.
- Allow minimum 60 minutes inside the temple, 30 minutes for reading gallery poetry.
- Buy combined ticket with Grand Palace (500 baht) online in advance to reduce queue time.

## Language and Community
Inside, formal royal Thai language is used, but you may hear Isan Lao from visiting monks and Teochew Chinese bargaining from shops in surrounding communities. Greet with "sawatdee krap/ka" and dress respectfully to honor this sacred site.

## Art, Culture, and Food
After paying respects, walk to Tha Maharaj Market for khao chae (rice in jasmine water, Bang Pa-In palace recipe), thong ek (golden egg yolk sweets), or iced tea with palm sugar. The old vendor "Mae Kim Heng" sells candied fruit in Mon recipes, reflecting Rattanakosin Island's diversity. During Songkran, there's a water blessing ceremony for the Emerald Buddha and shadow puppet performances.

## Families and Safety
- Children under 120 cm free admission. Must wear short or long sleeve shirts, no shorts above the knee.
- Nursing and diaper changing room behind Sala Racha Damnoen.
- Strollers must be checked at information desk; use shoulder cloths for sun protection if carrying babies.
- Please silence phones before entering the ordination hall and remind children to sit with folded legs or kneel when paying respects.
