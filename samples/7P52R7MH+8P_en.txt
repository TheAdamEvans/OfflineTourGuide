## Do's and Don'ts

**DO**:
- Try pad thai from street carts: 50-60 baht, tastes way better than tourist restaurants.
- Walk into "Soi Rambuttri": small bars, good coffee, charming old houses, owners who chat.
- Buy fresh fruit: ripe mango, papaya, pineapple, freshly cut 30-40 baht. Better than 7-Eleven snacks.
- Bargain politely: "Lot noi dai mai?" (Can you discount?) with a smile always works.

**DON'T**:
- Exchange money at booths: terrible rates. Banks or ATMs much better.
- Buy fake goods: 300-baht Gucci bag? Can get fined if caught.
- Trust "tuk-tuk 1-day tour 500 baht": usually take tourists to high-commission shops. Use Grab or buses instead.
- Take random pills offered: safety first, fun later.

## Street Food: Safe and Delicious

**Pad Thai Hoi Thod Jae Fai**: So famous there's a 2-hour queue. Authentic pad thai 60 baht, crispy oyster omelet 80 baht. If you're patient.

**Som Tam Mae Lam Jiak**: At the entrance. Spicy Lao papaya salad, grilled chicken, sticky rice. Locals eat here plenty. Fair prices 70-100 baht.

**Boat Noodles in the Alley**: If you walk into the alley beside Bob Bar, you'll spot a cart selling fish ball noodles. Rich broth 50 baht. Taxi drivers love it.

**Roti Gluay Near Bar Bar**: Crispy roti, fragrant banana, topped with chocolate and condensed milk. 40 baht. Sweet relief after tired walking.

## The Real Community Still Lives Here

If you walk 5 minutes from Khaosan toward "Talat Yot," you'll see authentic Phra Nakhon life. Old wooden houses, third-generation mom-and-pop shops, men's haircuts 80 baht, laundry 40 baht/kilo, ancient Chinese medicine family recipes.

**Thanon Tanao**: Behind Khaosan. Here are Thai dress shops for locals, funeral wreath shops, old temples, and an elementary school open 70 years. This is why Khaosan still has real lifeâ€”because the local community remains.
