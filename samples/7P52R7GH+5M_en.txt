**Na Phra Lan Road, Phra Borom Maha Ratchawang, Phra Nakhon, Bangkok**

Welcome, everyone. This is the Grand Palace, construction beginning with the founding of Rattanakosin in 1782 on the eastern bank of the Chao Phraya River, formerly Mon and Lao communities. We'll walk through white walls over 1,900 meters long to touch the living heritage of the Chakri Dynasty.

## History and Architecture
- Chakri Maha Prasat Throne Hall blends colonial Italian base with Thai prang-style roofs, symbolizing King Rama V's era of opening to Western knowledge.
- Temple of the Emerald Buddha enshrines the Emerald Buddha carved from green jade during the Lan Xang period, with ceremonial robe changes three times yearly by the King.
- Stucco reliefs and glazed tiles in the gallery corridor tell 178 scenes of the Ramakien, painters using traditional mineral pigments mixed with fish glue.

## Natural Landscape
This side of the Chao Phraya receives westerly winds that ventilate the ceremonial courtyard. Tamalan and Peeb trees attract bees for pollination, while the inner emerald pond shelters bronze-winged jacanas and silver barb fish that old Bangkokians used to catch and eat. Building roofs use tiles that shed water quickly, reducing moisture accumulation in rainy season.

## Language and Community
This is the birthplace of standard Thai and royal Thai used in ceremonies. Guides say "sawatdee krap" with wai gesture, but you'll still hear Pathum or Mon accents from senior staff. Please dress respectfully to honor the rituals and ancestors of Bangkok people.
