## Myeongdong Cathedral‚ÄîIsland of Silence Amid Consumerism

But wait. **One spire rises** between the shopping malls. Myeongdong Cathedral. Korea's first Gothic cathedral, completed in 1898.

This isn't just a religious building. **It's a sanctuary of Korea's democracy movement.** In 1987, students fleeing dictatorship took refuge here. Police couldn't enter the cathedral. Religious sanctuary, literally a "place of refuge."

Free entry. November afternoons, sunlight streams through stained glass casting red and blue light dancing on the floor. **The only place in Myeongdong where the noise disappears.** Sit and rest for 5 minutes. No tourists here. Everyone's too busy buying sheet masks.

## Street Food‚ÄîThe Real Highlight

Shopping? Honestly, online is cheaper. But **street food** is different. You can't buy this online.

**Hotteok**: Warm pancake with sweet syrup filling. ‚Ç©1,500. Crispy outside, molten sugar inside. Easy to burn your palate. Take small first bite and let it cool.

**Gyeran-ppang**: Bread with whole egg baked inside. ‚Ç©2,000. Winter snack. Sweet-salty, warm, portable.

**Tteokbokki**: Spicy rice cakes and fish cakes in gochujang sauce. ‚Ç©3,000-4,000. Korean soul food. Spice level adjustable‚Äî"medium" is **already quite spicy** for most foreigners. Start with mild.

**Bungeoppang**: Fish-shaped waffle with red bean or cream filling. ‚Ç©1,000. Korean street snack since 1930s. Winter only.

**Takoyaki** (yes, Japanese food): Popular in Myeongdong. Octopus balls with sauce. ‚Ç©4,000. Evidence of Hallyu-Japan fusion.

Street food budget: ‚Ç©10,000-15,000 per person will fill you up.

## Cosmetics Shopping‚ÄîIf You Really Must

Yes, Myeongdong is K-beauty mecca. But some tips:

1. **Compare prices**: Same product, different prices at different stores. Check 2-3 places. Olive Young (big chain) usually cheapest.
2. **Request samples**: Say "sample juseyo" and they'll give you some. Free. Don't hesitate.
3. **Tax refund**: Purchases over ‚Ç©30,000, foreigners can get tax refund. Bring passport.
4. **Don't be fooled**: "Traditional Korean herbal cream" is usually a brand created in the 2010s. Not traditional. But might work well.

Real tip? **Dongdemun Market or Daiso near Gangnam CGV are cheaper.** Myeongdong is convenient but premium priced.

## With Family

**Strollers**: Possible but crowded. Baby carriers recommended on weekends. Pedestrian-only, so no car worries.

**Restrooms**: Myeongdong Tourist Information Center (free, clean), Lotte Department Store Myeongdong (2nd floor), Starbucks (customer use but generally tolerant).

**Nursing rooms**: Lotte Young Plaza 5th floor, Myeongdong Station nursing room (clean and quiet).

**Kid-friendly food**: Gyeran-ppang, bungeoppang, cheese hot dogs safest. Tteokbokki is spicy, hotteok is hot‚Äîbe careful.

## Beyond Myeongdong‚ÄîQuieter Neighbors

**Namsan slope** (8 min walk): Escape Myeongdong noise to cable car. Forest trails and tranquility.

**Namdaemun Market** (10 min walk): 600-year-old traditional market. Wholesale prices, local atmosphere, kalguksu and mandu.

**Insadong** (15 min by subway): Traditional crafts, teahouses, galleries. Myeongdong's opposite‚Äîcalm and artistic.

**Deoksugung Stone Wall Path** (8 min walk): Seoul's most romantic walking path. Especially beautiful in fall foliage season (early-mid November).

## Honest Advice

Myeongdong is **Korea's Venice**. Tourist trap? Yes. Expensive? Often. Authentic? Questionable.

Yet **the energy is incredible**. Neon signs, street food smells, noise mixing 10 languages, K-pop blasting from speakers, smiles from staff handing out samples. This is **2020s Seoul**‚Äîchaotic, commercial, multicultural, unapologetically capitalist.

Koreans also go to Myeongdong. Not for shopping, but to say **"I've been to Myeongdong."** They go to enjoy the experience of being tourists.

So don't take it too seriously. Buy a hotteok, grab some samples, get swept up in the crowd, rest briefly at Myeongdong Cathedral, laugh and leave.
